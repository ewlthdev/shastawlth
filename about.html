tWLTH

The World Savings Vault on TRON


What is Commonwealth (tWLTH)?

Commonwealth is a global savings fund, and a P2P safety net. Whenever funds are deposited, a 10% dividend is paid proportionally and instantly to every holder. This micro-dividend is used to provide scalable, long term income stability to the group as a whole. tWLTH is a TRC20-compliant token and can be accepted and used as money.


What is the maximum supply of tWLTH?

Technically, there is no ‘maximum supply’ of tWLTH - however, there is a ‘Current Supply’ - this is determined solely by how much TRX is stored in the contract, and it is impossible to create tWLTH without the appropriate amount of TRX to back it stored in the contract.


Can I trade tWLTH on UniSwap?

tWLTH is not compatible with JustSwap, because while it is TRC20-compliant, the `transfer()` function has been modified to call "withdraw()", which means a UniSwap trade contract would end up with TRX that it doesn't know what to do with. This is because while the JustSwap contract would hold tokens, it too would also earn Dividends, and as such whenever the transfer function would be used by it in a trade, this problem of "stuck" TRX would occur.

In short: No.


What is the Commonwealth Vault?

The vault is the true market cap of Commonwealth, it is how much TRON is locked in the smart contract. The vault can never be changed, taken down, or modified. It is open source and verified on TronScan - we encourage public audit.

The Commonwealth vault only takes in TRON (TRX) and pays out TRX. If the price of TRX is $10 or $1000, the system will operate the same way. If the price of TRX goes up 10x, so will the price of tWLTH.


Why don't my tWLTH show in my wallet?

Commonwealth is a very new type of smart contract, which means sometimes it does not display perfectly with wallets. As long as you can see your funds on the official web wallet, they are safe.


What is the Price Floor?

The Price Floor contract is a smart contract that holds tWLTH. It can never sell, but it earns dividends when others buy or sell. It provides a guaranteed price floor. If you use Commonwealth on Ethereum Classic (ETC), you will know this feature as "RainMaker".
